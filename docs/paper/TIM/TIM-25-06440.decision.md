### **评议意见：**

**副主编：**
根据审稿人的评价，论文有几个重要方面需要重新审视，且必须解决多个疑虑。因此，本文应予以拒稿。

#### **审稿人 #1：**
* 图 9 中标注为“target（目标）”的曲线来源是什么？
* 图 11 显示 LSTMu22 的最佳验证损失值为 0.02，FRIKANh8u616 为 0.015，但这似乎与图 10 所示的情况不符（图 10 中 LSTMu22 的训练损失优于 FRIKANh8u616）。
* 建议增加关于所提仪器的实时实验验证细节，以及查找表（LUT）的来源和用法。你是如何将耗时从未使用 LUT 的 2.805 秒缩短到 0.114 秒的？

#### **审稿人 #3：**
本文针对电化学地震计（MET）频率响应中的非线性漂移问题，提出了一种基于物理信息 KAN 网络（PIKAN）和 QLFRS 注入的 FRIRNN 补偿方法。虽然整体工作表现出一定的技术合理性，但创新性有限，实验结构不够严谨且表述欠佳。需要解决以下问题：
1.  **物理约束的必要性：** 虽然对比了 GRU 和 LSTM 以证明物理约束的必要性，但作者未将 PIKAN 与原始 KAN（无物理约束）进行对比。此外，引入的奇对称性和正定性等特性的有效性缺乏实验验证。建议补充实验以充分证明所提策略的有效性。
2.  **对比模型的代表性：** FRIKAN 虽然在对比实验中优于其他三个网络，但对比模型数量有限且代表性不足。在 AI 领域，除了擅长时序数据的 RNN 和 LSTM，某些 CNN 架构和基于 Transformer 的模型也具备很强的预测和补偿能力。此外，文中提到的 RVTDCNN 虽然在功率放大器线性化方面表现良好，但作者未说明其是否为该领域的最优方法。建议补充并对比更多相关方法。
3.  **KAN 的应用现状：** 作者提到 KAN 是新方法。该方法此前在漂移补偿领域是否有应用？如果有，应在相关工作（Related Work）中补充并进行对比实验以突出优越性；如果没有，应明确说明本工作的开创性。
4.  **数据集细节：** 数据集由作者自行设计收集，但未提供总数据量、信号特征和领域特征等具体细节。收集的数据是否反映了地下应用场景的典型信号特征？能否代表大多数现实情况？需要说明模拟了哪些具体的现实场景。
5.  **环境稳健性：** 实验数据是在 25°C 室温下收集的。考虑到该方法应用于地下资源勘探和地震分析，硬件的实际工作环境可能与实验条件差异巨大。必须评估该方法在处理由环境变化引起的漂移或其他信号失真时是否具有足够的鲁棒性。
6.  **公开数据集：** 是否存在用于信号漂移补偿研究的公开数据集？如果作者自采数据缺乏代表性，应在其他可比的公开数据集上验证该方法。
7.  **相关工作补充：** 目前对领域内现有的基于深度学习的频率漂移补偿方法的介绍较少，建议补充。

#### **审稿人 #4：**
作者为电化学地震计提出了一种结合物理约束 KAN 框架与注入式补偿技术的方法。该想法具有一定的创新性，可能会引起同行的兴趣。然而，稿件目前缺乏学术期刊所要求的严谨性和系统性分析。所提技术的有效性和泛化性尚未通过定量的评估得到充分证明。强烈建议进行以下分析以加强论文质量：
1.  **激活函数对比：** 将所提 KAN 的 B 样条激活函数与其他常用激活函数对比，展示其性能优势。
2.  **物理约束的效果：** 评估 B 样条激活函数在不同物理约束下的模型表现：(i) 仅奇对称，(ii) 仅正定性，(iii) 奇对称与正定性结合，(iv) 无物理约束。定量结果应汇总在表格中以便对比。
3.  **频率注入的效果：** 对有无频率注入的 RNN 模型进行类似的对比研究，并报告定量性能差异。建议使用其他架构替代 RNN 与所提架构对比，以证明其有效性。
4.  **损失函数消融实验：** 进行损失函数的消融研究，报告使用：(i) 仅 MAE，(ii) 仅 AFMAE，(iii) MAE 与 AFMAE 结合的结果。
5.  **超参数敏感性分析：** 提供框架中主要超参数的敏感性分析，以证明模型的鲁棒性和稳定性。
6.  **数据划分与预处理：** 明确说明训练集、验证集和测试集的划分比例，并详细描述所使用的预处理技术。
7.  **与 SOTA（最先进）方法对比：** 纳入与相关前沿技术的对比。目前文中未提供此类参考文献，且评估仅限于基础模型（如 LSTM 和 GRU），不足以进行全面评估。
8.  **计算成本分析：** 目前证明计算效率的对比是不充分的。应在计算成本和复杂度方面与 SOTA 方法进行对比。

**最后，** 大多数结果仅以图表形式呈现，难以得出确切结论。强烈建议作者尽可能将关键发现汇总成表格，以便进行更清晰、客观的比较。

Comments:

Associate Editor: Based on the reviewers' comments, several important aspects need to be revisited, and multiple concerns must be addressed. Therefore, the article should be rejected.


Reviewer #1: .- What is the origin of the curves labeled "target" in Fig. 9?

.- Fig. 11 shows a best validation loss value for LSTMu22 of 0.02 and for FRIKANh8u616 of 0.015 which does not seem to coincide with what is shown in Fig. 10 which includes a better training loss for LSTMu22 than FRIKANh8u616.

.- It would be interesting to include details about the real-time experimental validation of the proposed instrument and the origin and use of the LUT. How do you go from 2.805 seconds without a LUT to 0.114 seconds?


Reviewer #3: The article addresses the issue of nonlinear drift in the frequency response of MET and proposes a compensation method based on a physically informed KAN network (PIKAN) and a QLFRS-injected FRIRNN. While the overall work demonstrates technical soundness, its innovation is limited, and it suffers from insufficiently structured experiments and poor presentation. The following issues need to be addressed.
1.The proposed PIKAN incorporates physical constraints, and comparisons with models such as GRU and LSTM are provided to demonstrate the necessity of these constraints. However, the authors did not compare PIKAN with the original KAN (without physical constraints). Additionally, the effectiveness of the introduced properties, such as odd symmetry and positivity, lacks experimental validation. It is recommended to supplement relevant experiments to fully demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed strategies.
2.The proposed FRIKAN achieves better results in comparative experiments with three other networks. However, the number of models compared is limited, and their representativeness is insufficient. In the field of artificial intelligence, besides RNN and LSTM networks, which are effective for time series data, certain CNN architectures and Transformer-based models also exhibit strong predictive and compensation capabilities. Furthermore, while the RVTDCNN mentioned in the text performs well in power amplifier linearization, the authors did not clarify whether it is the best method in that domain. It is recommended to supplement and compare with additional relevant methods.
3.The authors mention that KAN is a recently proposed method. Have there been any prior applications of such methods in the field of drift compensation? If so, they should be supplemented in the related work section, and comparative experiments should be conducted to highlight the superiority of the proposed method. If not, the pioneering nature of this work should be explicitly stated.
4.The dataset used in the study was designed and collected by the authors, but specific details such as the total data volume, signal characteristics, and domain-specific features are not provided. Does the collected data reflect the typical signal characteristics of underground application scenarios? If not, can the data represent most real-world situations? Clarification is needed regarding which specific real-world scenarios were simulated.
5.The experimental data were collected at an ambient temperature of 25°C. Considering the practical application scenarios of the proposed method, such as underground resource exploration and seismic analysis, the actual working environment of hardware may differ significantly from the data collection conditions. It is essential to evaluate whether the proposed method is sufficiently robust to handle drift or other signal distortions caused by varying environmental conditions.
6.Are there any publicly available datasets for signal drift compensation research and testing? If the authors' collected data lacks representativeness, the proposed method should be validated on other comparable public datasets.
7.The introduction to existing deep learning-based methods for frequency drift compensation in the current field is limited. It is recommended to supplement the related work section.


Reviewer #4: The authors propose a physics-constrained KAN framework with an injection-based compensation technique for electrochemical seismometers. The idea demonstrates a degree of novelty and is potentially of interest to the community. However, in its current form, the manuscript lacks the rigorous and systematic analysis expected of a journal publication. The effectiveness and generality of the proposed techniques are not sufficiently justified through quantitative evaluation. The following analyses are strongly recommended to substantially strengthen the manuscript:

1) Activation Function Comparison: Compare the proposed KAN B-spline activation with other commonly used activation functions to clearly demonstrate its performance advantage.
2) Effect of Physics Constraints: Evaluate the model performance under different physics-based constraints applied to the B-spline activation, namely: (i) odd symmetry alone, (ii) positivity alone, (iii) combined odd symmetry and positivity, and (iv) without any physics constraint. Quantitative results should be summarized in a table for clear comparison.
3) Effect of Frequency Injection: Conduct a similar comparative study for the frequency-injected RNN and the corresponding model without frequency injection, and report the quantitative performance differences. Instead of RNN, use other architectures and compare with proposed architecture to prove its effectiveness.
4) Loss Function Ablation Study: Perform an ablation study on the loss function by reporting results using: (i) MAE alone, (ii) AFMAE alone, and (iii) a combination of MAE and AFMAE.
5) Hyperparameter Sensitivity Analysis: Provide a sensitivity analysis covering all major hyperparameters involved in the proposed framework to demonstrate robustness and stability.
6) Dataset Split and Preprocessing: Clearly specify the percentage split used for training, validation, and testing, along with a detailed description of the preprocessing techniques employed.
7) Comparison with State-of-the-Art Methods: Include comparisons with relevant state-of-the-art techniques. At present, no references are provided for such comparisons, and the evaluation is limited to basic models (e.g., LSTM and GRU), which is insufficient for a comprehensive assessment.
8) Computational Cost Analysis: The current comparison used to justify computational efficiency is inadequate. The proposed method should be compared with state-of-the-art approaches in terms of computational cost and complexity.


Finally, most results are presented only in graphical form, making it difficult to draw definitive conclusions. The authors are strongly encouraged to summarize key findings in tabular form wherever possible to enable clearer and more objective comparison.
