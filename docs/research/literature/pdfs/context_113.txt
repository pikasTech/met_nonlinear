118: 2.1 Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks
119: Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks (KANs) replace the fixed activation
120: functions and matrix multiplications of MLPs with learnable spline-
121: based functions on network edges [ 28]. This activation-centric for-
122: mulation improves expressiveness and interpretability, often achiev-
123: ing comparable accuracy with fewer parameters and operations.Since their introduction, KANs have inspired extensive follow-up
124: work, including theoretical analyses [ 45,48], architectural exten-
125: sions (e.g., convolutional [9, 15], temporal [18], and Fourier-based
126: variants [ 21,49]), and applications across scientific modeling and
127: data-driven tasks [13, 19, 25, 46].
